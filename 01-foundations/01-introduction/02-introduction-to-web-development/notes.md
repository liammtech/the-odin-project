## Introduction

What do web developers do? In short, they build and maintain websites.

- Often work for clients trying to get their product/service onto the web
- Typically project-focused
- Involves collaborating with a team to coordinate the client's needs into the end
product
- Client could be a tech company, an organisation, or a government
- Could involve front-end, back-end, or full-stack development

Web dev could be a good profession for you if you like:?

- Solving logical problems
- Building useful things
- Experimenting with new technologies

Web developers are:

- In high demand
- Generally have a good work-life balance
- Command comfortable salaries

## Lesson overview

## Types of web developers

What do the terms front-end, back-end, and full-stack mean?

- Front end is the stuff you see in your browser; developers use HTML, CSS and JavaScript
to ensure the content is presented effectively

- Back end refers to the guts of the application, which live on a server; back-end devs
use programming languages like Java, Python, Ruby, and JavaScript to work with data

- Full stack developers are comfortable at both ends

## Types of careers

- Large tech companies: stringent hiring requirements, but offer excellent pay, benefits,
and opportunities

- Startups: like the wild west - slightly lower salaries and require longer hours, but
may also offer equity in the company and highly unique environments

- Freelancer: can command a strong hourly wage, and your own schedule - however, you're
responsible for getting your own work, managin billing, and being solely responsible (also
needs good people skills)

- Large, older companies: still need web developers - good work-life balance, pay, and
benefits, but move slower

## Tools of the trade

## Motivation

Learning to code is rewarding, but can be difficult. 

The strongest assets you can have:

- Desire to build
- Problem-solving mind
- Persistence in the face of setbacks

Web dev has a long history of successful developers with varying backgrounds, so people
tend to care more about what you've actually built than how you got there.
